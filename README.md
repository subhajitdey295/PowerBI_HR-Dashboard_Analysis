# PowerBI_HR-Dashboard_Analysis

✅ Project Overview: This repository showcases an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard designed to monitor workforce demographics, track attrition rates, and diagnose the core drivers behind employee turnover. By analyzing factors such as age, department, job role, and education field alongside employee job satisfaction scores, this dashboard helps HR leaders build targeted retention strategies and improve talent management.

✅ Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): The dashboard centralizes six primary operational and demographic metrics for high-level monitoring:
    ➡️ Total Employees: 1,470 (The total active workforce across departments).
    ➡️ Total Attrition: 237 (The headcount of employees who have left the company).
    ➡️ Attrition Rate: 16.12% (The percentage of overall turnover against total headcount).
    ➡️ Average Salary: 6.5K (The average compensation metric across the workforce).
    ➡️ Average Age: 37 Years (The average age of the employee base).
    ➡️ Average Years at Company: 7 Years (The typical tenure baseline for employees).
    ➡️ Gender Demographics: Active workforce split consisting of 588 Female and 882 Male employees.

✅ Business Challenge (Situation): The HR department lacked centralized visibility into why employee turnover was occurring. Scattered workforce records made it difficult to cross-reference attrition against demographics, tenure, and department-specific variables, preventing data-backed strategic planning to reduce exit rates.

✅ Solution Objective (Task): Design and build a comprehensive, interactive Power BI dashboard capable of tracking core employee KPIs and segmenting the 237 attrition cases across critical dimensions (Age, Education Field, Job Role, and Satisfaction Level) to unearth the primary drivers of workplace departures.

✅ Project Implementation (Action):
    ➡️ Data Modeling & ETL: Cleaned and modeled employee demographic datasets, separating active personnel from attrition records.
    ➡️ Visualization Engineering: Implemented an interactive dark-mode interface utilizing a variety of advanced charts: a line graph for tracking Attrition by Years at Company, a donut chart for Attrition by Education Field, and a structured bar graph for Attrition by Age Group.
    ➡️ Granular Cross-Filtering: Designed a detailed 100% stacked bar chart to cross-examine Attrition by Job Role and Job Satisfaction levels (ranging from scale 1 to 4), integrated alongside department filters (Human Resources, Research & Development, Sales).

✅ Measurable Business Outcomes (Result): Identified Critical Risk Groups: Pinpointed the 26-35 age bracket as having the highest attrition count (116 departures), calling for a revision of mid-level career growth initiatives.
    ➡️ Discovered Educational Trends: Revealed that employees with backgrounds in Life Sciences (91) and Medical (65) made up the largest share of departures.
    ➡️ Correlated Attrition with Job Roles: Isolated roles like Laboratory Technicians, Sales Executives, and Research Scientists are higher turnover sectors, with a notable concentration of low job satisfaction scores helping pinpoint toxic or high-burnout operational areas.

✅ Technology Stack:
    ➡️ Core Tool: Power BI Desktop (Data Modeling, Visualizations, Advanced DAX for Attrition % calculation, ETL Processing).
    ➡️ Skills Demonstrated: Relational Data Modeling, Demographic Profiling, Strategic HR Metric Design, and Interactive UI/UX Development.

✅ Conclusion & Value Delivered:
    ➡️ This analytics solution transforms standard workforce logs into a highly predictive corporate asset. Centralizing attrition trends, it allows leadership to pivot from reactive damage control to proactive retention planning. Armed with specific insights regarding at-risk age brackets and low-satisfaction job roles, management can deploy tailored engagement programs, benchmark salaries effectively, and optimize internal policies to lower the current 16.12% attrition rate.
